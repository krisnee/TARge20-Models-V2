Waterfall: Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages Disadvantages
➕ Waterfall model uses a clear and defined set of steps to follow when compared with other methodologies. ➖ Waterfall model follows a set of steps that always keep a team moving forward.
Unlike other methodologies that get used for development, the waterfall model doesn’t require specific training or certifications for employees or project managers. When you need to make a pivot after completing some or most of a project, then the waterfall model isn’t going to let you proceed.
One of the defining advantages of using the waterfall model is that it commits to the end goal, product, or deliverable from the beginning of the process. The waterfall model focuses on the internal processes of the work instead of looking at the client or end-user who gets involved with the project.
